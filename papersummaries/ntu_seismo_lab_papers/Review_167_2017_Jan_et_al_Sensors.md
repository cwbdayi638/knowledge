Abstract: Following the recent establishment of a high-density seismic network equipped with
low-cost micro-electro-mechanical system (MEMS) P-wave-alert-device (P-Alert) by the earthquake
early warning (EEW) research group at the National Taiwan University, a large quantity of
strong-motion records from moderate-magnitude earthquakes (ML > 6) around Taiwan has been
accumulated. Using a data preprocessing scheme to recover the dynamic average embedded within
the P-Alert data, we adopted an automatic baseline correction approach for the P-Alert accelerograms
to determine the coseismic deformation (Cd). Comparisons between the Cd values determined using
global positioning system (GPS) data, strong-motion records from the P-Alert network, and data from
the Taiwan Strong Motion Instrumentation Program (TSMIP) demonstrates that the near-real-time
determination of Cd values (>2 cm), which provide crucial information for seismic hazard mitigation,
is possible using records from low-cost MEMS accelerometers.
Keywords: low-cost MEMS accelerometers; earthquake permanent displacement; seismic hazard
mitigation; P-Alert
