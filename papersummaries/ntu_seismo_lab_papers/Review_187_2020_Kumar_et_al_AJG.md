Abstract
Development of earthquake early warning system (EEWS) is in advanced stage in different parts of the world including India.
The success of EEWS for mitigating seismic risk and saving human lives has been well documented in Mexico, Japan, and
Taiwan, where the alert is issued to the public. Taking advantage of the recorded ground motion data from the network of Institute
of Seismological Research (ISR), India, with magnitude range 3.0â€“5.2, we investigated correlations between various ground
motion parameters like peak ground acceleration (PGA), peak ground velocity (PGV), peak ground displacement (PGD), Pa,
Pv, Pd, and Ï„c.Three- to 5-s time windows are considered to measure Pa, Pv, Pd, and Ï„c from the vertical component of the
waveforms. Linear regression analysis is performed at various time steps (3- to 5-s time interval). The results show that
considering 4- and 5-s windows exhibits good relationships compared with the 3-s window, which shows more scattering.
These empirical relationships using Ï„c and M as well as Pd and M are very helpful in determining the earthquake magnitude
and subsequently taking steps toward risk assessment.
