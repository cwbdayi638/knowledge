1 Introduction
Direct ocean injection of CO2 is one of several approaches
under consideration to sequester carbon dioxide in order to
stabilize increased atmospheric CO2. A potential CO2
storage option is to inject captured CO2 directly into the
deep ocean at depths deeper than -1,000 m or more, where
most of CO2 would be isolated from the atmosphere for
centuries. This can be achieved by transporting CO2 via
pipelines or ships to an ocean storage site, where CO2 is
injected into the water column of the ocean or at the sea
floor. At typical pressures and temperatures, CO2 exists as
gas in the ocean at a depth of approximately -500 m and
as a liquid below that depth. Between depths of about 500 and -2,700 m, liquid CO2 is lighter than sea water
